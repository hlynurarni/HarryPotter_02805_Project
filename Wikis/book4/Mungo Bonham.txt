'''Saint Mungo Bonham''' ([[1560]] – [[1659]]) was a [[Wizardkind|wizard]] and [[Healer]] who founded [[St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries]] in the [[1600s]].
==Behind the scenes==
*In reality, Saint Mungo is the commonly used name for [[Wikipedia:Saint Mungo|Saint Kentigern]] of the [[Wikipedia:Catholic|Catholic church]] who lived in the sixth century [[Scotland]] and [[England]]. He was also known as a founder of the [[Wikipedia:Glasgow Cathedral|Glasgow Cathedral]]. A Feast day for St Mungo, also known as St Mungo's Day,<ref>'''[http://www.the-leaky-cauldron.org/myleaky/events/503 The-Leaky-Cauldron.org]''': ''St Mungo's Day''</ref> is observed on the [[13 January|13th of January]] every year after he was canonised.
*Interestingly, Lula Landry, the victim in [[J. K. Rowling|Robert Galbraith]]'s first Cormoran Strike novel ''[[Wikipedia:The Cuckoo's Calling|The Cuckoo's Calling]]'', lives in the fictional Kentigern Gardens, in [[Wikipedia:Mayfair|Mayfair]]. Kentigern, as noted above, in the real name of the real-life Saint Mungo.
*Mungo also shares part of his name with [[Helena Bonham Carter]], the actress who portrays [[Bellatrix Lestrange]] in the [[Harry Potter (film series)|Harry Potter films]].

==Etymology==
The name "Mungo" is possibly derived from Welsh ''mwyn'' "gentle, kind". This was a nickname of the 6th-century Saint Kentigern.

The surname "Bonham" is an Anglicisation of the French surname "Bonhomme", which itself is derived from the Old French "bon homme" meaning "good man".
