'''"Magic is Might"''' was the motto of the the [[British Ministry of Magic|Ministry of Magic]] while it was under the control of [[Tom Riddle|Lord Voldemort]] in [[1997]] and [[1998]]. It represented the idea that [[wizardkind|wizards and witches]] are inherently superior to [[Non-magic people|Muggles]], and, as such, are entitled to rule over them.<ref name="DH12">{{DH|B|12}}</ref><ref name="DH18">{{DH|B|18}}</ref>

==Known Uses==
This phrase was engraved at the base of a [[Magic is Might statue|statue]] that was installed in the [[Atrium]] of the Ministry of Magic Headquarters after Voldemort took power. Replacing the [[Fountain of Magical Brethren]], the statue featured a gigantic witch and wizard seated atop thrones made up of tiny, naked, stupid- and ugly-looking Muggles.<ref name="DH12">{{DH|B|12}}</ref>

==Similar Mottos==
[[Hermione Granger]] compared "Magic is Might" to "[[For the Greater Good]]", the motto [[Gellert Grindelwald]] used to justify his actions in the [[Global wizarding war|European Wizarding Revolution]].<ref name="DH18">{{DH|B|18}}</ref>

==Behind the scenes==
*Similar slogans are also used in other prominent works of fiction such as the party slogans in the dystopian novel [[Wikipedia:Nineteen Eighty-Four|Nineteen Eighty-Four]] by George Orwell: "War is Peace", "Freedom is Slavery", and "Ignorance is Strength".
*In the {{DH|F1}}, the Muggles are clothed, the wizard and witch standing rather than seated, and the statue white rather than black.<ref>{{DH|F1}}</ref>
