'''Hengist of Woodcroft''' was a [[Middle Ages|Medieval]] [[England|English]] [[Wizardkind|wizard]], best known as the founder of the village of [[Hogsmeade]] in [[Scotland]], the only all-wizarding village in [[Great Britain]].

==Biography==
Hengist was born in the [[Middle Ages]], no earlier than [[First millennium A.D.|982]],<ref name="birthdate" /> in [[Woodcroft]], [[England|Gloucestershire]]. In his youth, Hengist attended [[Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry]], where he was [[Sorting ceremony|sorted]] into [[Hufflepuff]].<ref name="pottermore" /> He might have been taught by Helga Hufflepuff herself.

[[File:Hengist of Woodcroft USAcard.jpg|thumb|left|Hengist founding Hogsmeade]]

Hengist is traditionally credited with the founding of the village of [[Hogsmeade]] after being driven from his home by [[Non-magic people|Muggle]] persecutors. According to popular lore, Hengist used the [[Three Broomsticks Inn]] as a home.<ref name="PSvg">{{PS|G}}</ref>

Hengist died at some point during the [[Middle Ages]]. Remembered for his founding of [[Hogsmeade]], he was immortalised in a [[Chocolate Frog Card]]<ref name="PSvg" /> Hengist of Woodcroft's Chocolate Frog Card was one of the very first collected by [[Harry Potter]].<ref name="ps6">{{PS|B|6}}</ref> There was also a bust of Hengist on the [[Fourth-floor corridor|Fourth Floor Corridor]] of [[Hogwarts Castle]].<ref name="POAG">{{POA|G}}</ref>

On [[1 September]], [[1991]], [[Prefect]] [[Gabriel Truman]] welcomed the [[Hufflepuff]] [[first year]]s to [[Hufflepuff Basement]] with a short speech in which he mentioned Hengist of Woodcroft as one of the many notable Hogwarts students who were [[Sorting ceremony|sorted]], like them, into Hufflepuff.<ref name="pottermore" />

==Etymology==
The name "Hengist" means "stallion" in Germanic. Hengist and his brother Horsa were the leaders of the first Germanic settlers in Britain. Hengist established a kingdom in [[Kent]] in the [[First millennium A.D.|5th century]].
